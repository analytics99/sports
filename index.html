Season Narrative 2025

The 2025 Formula One season was the 76th running of the World Championship, contested over 24 Grands Prix from Australia in March to Abu Dhabi in December. The season ended four years of Max Verstappen dominance, as Lando Norris claimed his maiden title by just two points in the season finale at Abu Dhabi — one of the closest finishes in modern F1 history.


McLaren were the dominant force throughout the year, with their two drivers combining for 14 race wins out of 24. Verstappen fought brilliantly despite Red Bull's relative midfield car, winning 8 races — more than any other driver. The season saw Lewis Hamilton make his high-profile switch to Ferrari, Kimi Antonelli become the youngest driver to lead a race and set fastest lap on debut for Mercedes, and an unexpected podium from Williams' Carlos Sainz.


Key controversies included three post-race disqualifications — Leclerc, Hamilton and Gasly in China, Hülkenberg in Bahrain, and Norris & Piastri in Las Vegas. McLaren clinched the Constructors' title with six rounds to spare, amassing a record 833 points.
